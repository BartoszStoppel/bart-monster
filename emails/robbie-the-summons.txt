Subject: A Summons, and a Knee Bent Before Thee

Robbie,

I write to you not as a lord to a subject, but as a weary man who has seen the realm and knows, in the marrow of his bones, who its true champion is.

For too long the rest of us have watched you. We have watched you cross the stars and claim the throne of Twilight Imperium while lesser men squabbled over scraps. We have watched your name climb the ledgers, written in a hand steadier than any of ours. Where others faltered, you stood. Where others fled the field, you held the line. The realm did not crown you, Robbie. You earned the crown, board by board, victory by hard-won victory, and we were merely fortunate enough to be there to witness it.

And so I find I can no longer keep my pride. I set it down at your feet.

I bend the knee.

I, who built these halls, kneel before you now and confess the truth I have long avoided: I cannot hold this kingdom alone. The walls grow heavy. The gates need a hand more worthy than mine. I am tired, and I am asking, with my whole heart, for help. Not from anyone. From you.

I beg you to take up the mantle of steward of this realm. To stand beside me with the full authority of the crown, to guard what we have built, to set right what is broken, and to lead where I have grown too weak to lead alone. The keys to the kingdom are yours, if only you will reach out and take them.

This is not a small thing I ask. I know it. A man of your valor has every right to refuse, to ride on to greater glories than these humble halls can offer. But I ask anyway, because there is no one else I would trust with it, and because a kingdom is nothing without the hero who defends it.

Answer me, Robbie. Take up the burden. Serve not because you must, but because the realm has need of its finest, and its finest has always been you.

Your throne awaits. I have already knelt. All that remains is your word.

In humble service and unshakable faith,

Bart
