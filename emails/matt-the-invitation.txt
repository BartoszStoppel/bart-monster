Subject: An Invitation to Raise These Walls With Me

Matt,

I write to you as one builder to another, with a request I have carried in my chest for some time and can no longer keep silent.

This realm of ours was raised stone by stone. Every hall, every gate, every quiet corner where our friends gather to weigh their games and crown their champions, all of it was laid by hand, late into the night, often by lamplight alone. I am proud of it. But I have come to know a truth that pride cannot argue with: no keep worth defending was ever built by a single mason. The walls have grown taller than one pair of hands can tend, and I find myself longing for the one thing I have always lacked here, a craftsman to stand at the workbench beside me.

So I come to you, hat in hand, and I bend the knee.

I am asking you to take up the hammer and the chisel and build this realm with me. Not as a guest who passes through, but as an architect of it, with your hands on the very stones, your name carved into the foundation alongside mine. I want your craft. I want your judgment. I want the things you would build that I have never thought to imagine.

I will not pretend this is a small thing. To build is to bleed a little, to wrestle with the work until it yields, to care about a thing enough to stay when it would be easier to leave. But I have seen how you work, and I know the quality of what you make, and there is no one I would rather hand a key to the workshop. The forge is lit. The plans are spread upon the table. There is a place at the bench that has been empty too long, and it was always meant to be yours.

Come and build with me, Matt. Lay your stone beside mine. Let us raise these walls together, higher than either of us could raise them alone, and leave behind something worth the labor.

The workshop door is open. I am only waiting on your word to begin.

With deep respect and an outstretched hand,

Bart
